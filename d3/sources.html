Sources

Census Data

Data from all of the census and American Community Survey variables can be found at Census.gov However, since the outcome of each census leads to adjustments in tract boundaries, comparing across multiple years can be tricky. The Longitudinal Tract Database (Brown, Xu & Stults) does all the heavy lifting of interpolating 2000 data into 2010 geography for direct comparison.

Damage Data

Individual address damage estimates for over 100,000 properties is available from the Post-Katrina Damage Assessment, on the City of New Orleans Open Data Portal. This damage was averaged out by linking each address to a census tract using the US Census Geocoder API

Gentrification Methodology

Our methodology for quantitatively defining gentrification is drawn from Governing.com's Gentrification in America Report, and Lance Freeman's Displacement or Succession?.
